Kiran Reddy Bhumireddy

MSCS @ NYU Courant | B.Tech CS @ IIIT Hyderabad | Ex-Researcher @ RRC

New York, NY

I am a Master’s student in Computer Science at New York University’s Courant Institute, where I work at the intersection of distributed systems and artificial intelligence. Before joining NYU, I graduated with Honours with a B.Tech. in Computer Science and Engineering from the International Institute of Information Technology, Hyderabad.

My primary interest is Agentic Engineering, the discipline of building reliable, production-ready systems around large language models. While modern LLMs are remarkably capable, transforming them into dependable software requires careful systems design, robust infrastructure, and thoughtful engineering. That challenge is what excites me the most.

I began my engineering journey as a Student Researcher at the Robotics Research Center under Dr. Spandan Roy, where I worked on asynchronous telemetry pipelines and low-latency Kalman filtering algorithms in C++. I later joined Ambitio as a Software Engineer, building scalable backend systems and AI-powered products.

More recently, my work has focused on distributed systems and AI infrastructure. Some of the projects I’ve built include a Local-First AI Inference Gateway for running and orchestrating local language models, and a sharded transactional database implementing Multi-Paxos for fault-tolerant replication and consensus. I enjoy designing systems that are resilient, scalable, and practical to operate.

Outside of work and academics, I’m an enthusiastic open-source contributor and enjoy exploring the rapidly evolving ecosystem of local AI models and developer tooling. I like turning architectural ideas into working software, using projects as a way to deepen my understanding of distributed systems, fault tolerance, and modern AI infrastructure.

I also write technical articles on Medium, where I share deep dives, implementation notes, and lessons learned from building systems.
